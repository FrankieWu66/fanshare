## How You Actually Make Money

FanShare uses a linear bonding curve: `price = base_price + slope × tokens_sold`. As more people buy, the price goes up for every buyer after you.

The profit loop:

1. **Oracle index rises** — strong real-game stats increase the player's index price via the 4-pillar formula
2. **Spread signals undervaluation** — if market hasn't caught up yet, spread goes negative (market < index), flagging the token as undervalued
3. **Market price follows** — other traders reading the spread buy in, pushing the bonding curve price up
4. **Early holders profit** — you bought at a lower curve position, so price appreciation is realized when you sell

No dividends. No payouts. **Value is price appreciation, driven by performance signal — not hype.**

---

## Why You Cannot Profit by Buying Then Immediately Selling

This is the most important mechanic to understand.

When you buy n tokens, you walk **up** the bonding curve — paying increasing prices for each token.
When you sell n tokens, you walk **down** the same curve — receiving decreasing prices.

These are mathematically identical. Buying then immediately selling returns exactly what you paid, minus the trading fee on both legs.

**Net result of instant round-trip: guaranteed loss of ~2× fee (roughly 3% total).**

Spread is not profit sitting in your pocket. It is information — the gap between what the market thinks and what the oracle says. You can only capture spread if you were **already holding** when others pushed the price up, or if you correctly anticipated that the oracle would rise.

---

## Why Hold Instead of Sell Immediately

The bonding curve rewards conviction holders:

- Buy early at low curve position (cheap)
- Player performs consistently → oracle index rises → market follows → curve moves up
- Sell later at higher curve position (expensive for next buyer)

**The longer your read stays right, the more you compound.** Flipping after one good game captures one night. Holding a structurally undervalued player through a month of strong performances captures the full market re-rating.

## The Edge for Someone With Real Basketball Knowledge

Sports betting: binary bet that expires. Sportsbook sets the line based on sharp money — your knowledge is one voice in a system designed to extract margin.

FanShare is structurally different:

- **Your edge is durable.** A correct read on a player compounds over time on the bonding curve. Early + right = the most profitable position.
- **The oracle is transparent.** You can see exactly why a price moved. Stats went up → index went up → spread signaled undervaluation → market followed. No black box.
- **Spread is the skill layer.** The gap between oracle index and market price is the information gap the knowledgeable fan can exploit.
- **No house edge.** Platform charges a trading fee — that is the cost of liquidity, not the cost of losing to a better-capitalized opponent. You're trading against other fans.

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a dividend product — no cash payouts tied to player performance
- Not a prediction market — no binary outcome, no expiry, continuous price
- Not a collectible — value comes from performance, not from scarcity or rarity
- Not speculation-proof — FanShare can attract speculators, but the stat-backed oracle means speculation has a tether to reality that pure meme coins don't have
